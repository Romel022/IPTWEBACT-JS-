PALAWAN BEARCAT

Scientific Name: Arctictis binturong whitei

Location: Palawan

Status: Possibly Endagered

Description:
The bearcat subspecies known as the Palawan bearcat, or Arctictis binturong whitei, is an animal only found in the Philippine - island of Palawan. These enormous, arboreal beasts have a striking look that incorporates features of both cats and bears. A long, prehensile tail, shaggy black fur, - and a white face with distinguishing facial markings are the main characteristics of the Palawan bearcat. They spend a lot of time in trees and have powerful claws, - Making them great climbers. Frugivorous animals, Palawan bearcat eats primarily fruits but also small mammals, birds, and insects. Due to habitat loss, deforestation, hunting for food and pet trade, as well as habitat degradation, the Palawan bearcat is officially classified as vulnerable species To safeguard their natural habitats and guarantee the survival of this distinctive and endagered subspecies, conservation initiatives are currently under progress.
