PHILIPPINE TARSIER

Scientific Name: Carlito syrichta / Tarsius syrichta

Location: Bohol, Samar, Leyte, and Mindanao

Status: Endagered

Description:
Tarsius syrichta, a small dwelling primate, also known as the Philippine tarsier, which is only found in the Philippines. It is one of the tiniest primates in the world and is renowned for its large eyes, which each eye are around the same size of its brain. These nocturnal animals are known for their excellent leaping abilities and long fingers and toes, in which help them navigate through the dense forests. Bohol, Samar, Leyte, and Mindanao are the main Philippine islands where the Philippine tarsier can be found. These little primates are both inscetivorous - and carnivorous, relying mostly on insects such as grasshoppers and crickets for food. They are also very vocal and are capable to make wide range of vocalizations. Sadly, it is also regarded as a vulnerable species and is protected by the Philippine law as a result of habitat degradation and illicit pet trade. To assist safeguard - these unsual and fascinating creatures and preserve their natural habitats, conservation efforts and initiatives have been implemented.
